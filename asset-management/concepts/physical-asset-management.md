# Physical Asset Management

## Overview
[[physical-asset-management]] focuses on the stewardship of fixed or non-current physical assets through their full life cycle. The sources treat it as a strategic discipline, not just a maintenance function.

## What the sources emphasize
The two 2010 books frame physical asset management as whole-life decision-making under risk, service, and value constraints. The 2020 empirical paper reinforces this with a core-practices model built around strategy and planning, risk management, lifecycle delivery, asset information, and asset review.

## Why it matters
This concept is narrower than the broader [[asset-management]] page but often more concrete for infrastructure and engineering organizations.

A recent AR/MR comparison paper makes that concreteness explicit by tying technology selection to PAM realities such as PPE compatibility, ruggedisation, offline work, long-duration inspections, and integration with digital workflows. In practice, this means PAM is not just about deciding to "go digital"; it is about choosing tools that fit the environment, asset risks, and work patterns.

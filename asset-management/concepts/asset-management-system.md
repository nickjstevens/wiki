# Asset Management System

## Definition
An asset management system is the structured set of policies, objectives, processes, and controls used to direct and coordinate asset-management activity.

## Important distinction
The broader discipline of [[asset-management]] is larger than the management system for asset management. [[iso-55001]] defines system requirements, [[iso-55002]] gives application guidance, and PAS 55 provides an earlier physical-asset predecessor. None of them, on their own, exhaust the full practice of asset management.

## Practical meaning
A good asset management system aligns context, leadership, planning, support, operational control, performance evaluation, and improvement. The ONR guidance shows that in high-consequence settings the system also needs to support whole-of-life risk thinking, preservation, replacement, configuration management, and supply-chain control.

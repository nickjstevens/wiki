# Asset Management Maturity

## Overview
[[asset-management-maturity]] describes how far an organization has developed its asset-management capabilities beyond baseline conformance. The source set is explicit that conformance with [[iso-55001]] does not by itself guarantee that all stakeholder value has been realized.

## What it contributes
The GFMAM position statement argues that maturity should be understood in terms of characteristics and business benefits, not just box-ticking. ISO 55000 reinforces this by introducing indicators of maturity alongside principles and outcomes.

## Why it matters
This concept keeps the wiki from equating compliance with capability. It connects review, learning, and capability development back to [[value-realization]].

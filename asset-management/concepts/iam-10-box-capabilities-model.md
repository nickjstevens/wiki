# IAM 10-box Capabilities Model

## Overview
The IAM 10-box capabilities model is a practical framework for organizing the full asset-management discipline beyond minimum management-system conformance.

## Purpose
The model gives a structure for viewing the field as an interconnected set of capabilities, from context and governance through delivery, information, risk, review, and outcomes. The additional source set makes that structure feel less idiosyncratic: the GFMAM Landscape and the empirical core-practices paper emphasize many of the same capability groupings.

## Why it matters
It is useful as a navigation backbone for this wiki because it shows how [[asset-management]], [[asset-management-system]], [[lifecycle-delivery]], [[asset-information]], [[risk-management]], [[asset-review]], and [[value-realization]] fit together.

## Ten capabilities at a glance
- purpose and context
- leadership and governance
- organization and people
- strategy and planning
- asset management decision-making
- life cycle delivery
- information management
- risk
- review and continual improvement
- value and outcomes

# Asset Management

## Definition
The IAM Anatomy defines asset management as the coordinated activity of an organization to realize value from assets. The broader source set reinforces that this is a whole-organization discipline rather than a synonym for maintenance or asset registers.

## Core meaning in this wiki
Asset management links purpose, governance, planning, delivery, information, risk, and performance across the life of assets and asset systems. ISO 55000 adds a standards-based explanation of principles, outcomes, and benefits, while the physical-asset texts make the whole-life and infrastructure-centered implications more concrete.

## Key principles
Across the sources, recurring themes are outcomes focus, responsiveness to organizational context, alignment across the organization, a whole-of-life perspective, and the importance of evidence, leadership, and review.

# Asset Management Decision-Making

## Definition
Asset-management decision-making is the structured process of choosing among options for acquisition, operation, maintenance, inspection, renewal, or disposal under uncertainty and constraint.

## Why it sits at the center
The sources treat decision-making as a core capability where purpose, value, risk, information, and lifecycle thinking are turned into actual choices. ISO 55001's updated treatment and the IAEA/INSAG material both reinforce that this is not a side activity; it is central to the discipline.

## What good practice looks like
Good decisions are consistent, proportionate, documented, and aligned with the organization's value framework. They weigh cost, risk, performance, service outcomes, and stakeholder effects rather than optimizing a single metric in isolation.

## Why integrated risk matters
The nuclear-sector sources are especially explicit that decision quality improves when risk information is integrated rather than bolted on afterwards. That makes this page tightly linked to [[risk-management]] and [[asset-information]].

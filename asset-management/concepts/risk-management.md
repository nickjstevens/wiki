# Risk Management

## Definition
Risk management in this domain is the systematic treatment of uncertainty affecting organizational objectives, asset performance, service outcomes, safety, and compliance.

## Why it matters
Risk is foundational to prioritization, intervention timing, and resource allocation. The broader source set sharpens this further by showing that risk should be integrated into decision processes, not handled as a disconnected assurance exercise.

## Practical use
The sources support a structured approach that includes identifying, assessing, prioritizing, treating, and monitoring risk. The nuclear-sector sources also stress whole-of-life risks, preservation and replacement choices, and the integration of operational, financial, and safety consequences.

# Institute of Asset Management

## Overview
The Institute of Asset Management (IAM) is a central professional body in this source set, shaping the language, models, and practice of infrastructure asset management through its Anatomy, its role in [[pas-55]], and its participation in the wider international community represented by GFMAM.

## Role in the discipline
IAM acts as both explainer and organizer of the field. It provides a structured language for [[asset-management]], clarifies the relationship between the broader discipline and the [[asset-management-system]], and promotes the [[iam-10-box-capabilities-model]] as a practical organizing framework.

## Why it matters
IAM helps translate broad ideas such as value, lifecycle thinking, governance, and risk into a coherent discipline. It also sits near the historical bridge from [[pas-55]] to the ISO 55000 family and remains closely connected to the broader consensus work of [[global-forum-on-maintenance-and-asset-management]].

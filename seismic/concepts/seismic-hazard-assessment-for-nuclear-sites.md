# Seismic hazard assessment for nuclear sites

Nuclear-site hazard assessment emphasizes defensible probabilistic characterization because downstream design and qualification decisions are sensitive to both low-probability tail behavior and the way hazard is translated into analysis input.

## Why it matters

- Hazard assumptions propagate into floor spectra, component qualification, and design margins.
- Nuclear projects need stronger traceability from site characterization to structural demand than conventional projects.
- Hazard modeling also affects how synthetic or spectrum-matched records should be interpreted.

## Evidence from this ingest batch

- Tromans et al. provide a case study of probabilistic seismic hazard assessment for a new-build nuclear power plant site in the UK.
- [[asce-sei-43-05]] gives the design-criteria context that turns hazard outputs into target seismic demands.
- The nonstationary ground-motion generation paper shows one route for converting spectral targets into records for analysis.

## Engineering takeaway

Hazard assessment for nuclear sites is not a front-end checkbox. It is a governing input whose uncertainty and conservatism need to stay visible all the way through analysis and qualification.

# Modal combination methods and floor response spectra

Modal combination methods aggregate modal maxima in response-spectrum analysis, while floor response spectra translate structural amplification into demand measures for subsystems and equipment.

## Why it matters

- Combination rules determine how modal peaks are assembled into design demand.
- Floor spectra are often where building response becomes qualification input for secondary systems.
- The topic is especially central to [[nuclear-facility-seismic-analysis-and-design-criteria]].

## Evidence from this ingest batch

- The Brookhaven/NRC paper evaluates modal combination methods for seismic response-spectrum analysis.
- Jiang et al. present a direct spectra-to-spectra method for generating floor response spectra.
- ASCE 4-16 is the obvious standards backdrop for how such procedures are used in safety-related analysis.

## Engineering takeaway

Even when linear modal methods are appropriate, details of modal correlation, closely spaced modes, and floor-spectrum generation are not bookkeeping issues; they shape the actual qualification demand passed to supported systems.

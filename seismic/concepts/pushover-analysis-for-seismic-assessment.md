# Pushover analysis for seismic assessment

Pushover analysis is a nonlinear static procedure used to approximate structural capacity and likely yielding sequence under increasing lateral demand. This ingest batch emphasizes both its usefulness and its limits.

## Why it matters

- It remains attractive for assessment because it is much cheaper than full nonlinear response-history analysis.
- Its weaknesses become more important when torsion, bidirectional excitation, or degrading non-ductile behavior dominate.
- It should be interpreted alongside [[nonlinear-seismic-assessment-methods]] rather than treated as a universal answer.

## Evidence from this ingest batch

- Shakeri and Ghorbani propose a pushover procedure for buildings with bi-axial eccentricity under bidirectional excitation.
- Sharma et al. tie pushover ideas to full-scale RC behavior in a non-seismically detailed structure.
- NIST GCR 10-917-5 provides the broader practitioner guidance on when nonlinear procedures are informative and when judgment is required.

## Engineering takeaway

Pushover is most credible when the expected mechanism is reasonably coherent. As torsion, higher-mode effects, bidirectional demand, and detailing deficiencies increase, confidence should shift toward richer nonlinear analysis and stronger experimental calibration.

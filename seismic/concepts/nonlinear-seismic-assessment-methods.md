# Nonlinear seismic assessment methods

Nonlinear seismic assessment methods include nonlinear static procedures, nonlinear response-history analysis, and intensity-scaling frameworks such as incremental dynamic analysis. This batch repeatedly presents them as complementary rather than interchangeable.

## Why it matters

- Method choice controls what failure modes can be seen and what uncertainty is hidden.
- Different methods are differently sensitive to record selection, damping assumptions, and hysteretic modeling.
- This page is the bridge between procedural tools like [[pushover-analysis-for-seismic-assessment]] and design applications like [[seismic-design-of-steel-moment-frames]].

## Evidence from this ingest batch

- NIST GCR 10-917-5 gives practitioner-oriented guidance on nonlinear analysis for design.
- Hariri-Ardebili et al. compare time-history, incremental dynamic, and endurance-time methods for steel moment frames.
- The RC fragility paper uses incremental dynamic analysis to characterize collapse-related vulnerability.
- The bi-axial pushover paper highlights where nonlinear static procedures need enhancement.

## Engineering takeaway

The key trade-off is between cost and observability. Simpler procedures are attractive, but the more the response depends on higher modes, bidirectional effects, degradation, or collapse progression, the more the analysis method needs to expose path dependence explicitly.

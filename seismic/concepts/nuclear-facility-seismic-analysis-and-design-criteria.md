# Nuclear facility seismic analysis and design criteria

Nuclear seismic work uses a tighter chain between hazard definition, structural analysis, subsystem qualification, and performance target than ordinary building design. This ingest batch adds the first substantial standards spine for that chain.

## Why it matters

- Nuclear facilities are driven by safety-related performance requirements and explicit qualification logic.
- The distinction between analysis requirements and design criteria is sharper than in ordinary building work.
- Embedded structures, floor spectra, damping treatment, and hazard definition all recur as integrated issues rather than isolated topics.

## Evidence from this ingest batch

- [[asce-sei-4-16]] anchors seismic analysis of safety-related nuclear structures.
- [[asce-sei-43-05]] anchors seismic design criteria for nuclear facilities.
- Brookhaven/NRC papers add applied context for soil pressures and response-spectrum procedures.
- The direct floor-spectra paper shows the subsystem-demand side of nuclear analysis workflows.

## Engineering takeaway

Nuclear seismic practice is best understood as a systems workflow: hazard characterization feeds structural demand estimation, which feeds floor or support spectra, which then feeds qualification of structures, systems, and components under explicitly defined performance targets.

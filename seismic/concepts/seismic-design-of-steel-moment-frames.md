# Seismic design of steel moment frames

Steel moment-frame seismic design depends on matching analytical assumptions, member proportioning, and connection detailing to an intended ductile inelastic mechanism.

## Why it matters

- Code provisions only work when the connection and panel-zone behavior are compatible with the intended mechanism.
- Nonlinear assessment can expose gaps between nominal compliance and actual collapse resistance.
- This topic links design rules to the analysis choices summarized in [[nonlinear-seismic-assessment-methods]].

## Evidence from this ingest batch

- [[aisc-341-05]] provides system-level seismic provisions for structural steel buildings.
- [[aisc-358-05]] provides prequalified moment-frame connection guidance.
- Hariri-Ardebili et al. compare time-history, incremental dynamic, and endurance-time analysis methods for steel moment frames.
- NIST's technical brief frames how practicing engineers should use nonlinear analysis in design.

## Engineering takeaway

The persistent lesson is that ductility is not a line-item property. It emerges from the combined behavior of frame topology, member hierarchy, connection qualification, and the analysis method used to infer performance.

# Influence of Seismic Strain Stress on Evolution Law of Microcracks in Concrete TPB Tests Using AE Technology

## Abstract / source clipping

Since the fracture properties and failure modes of concrete are deeply rate-dependent, changes in crack resistance and failure mechanism of concrete under seismic strain rates can provide critical insights for structural safety withstanding seismic load. This study explored the dynamic fracture characteristics and micro-mechanisms of concrete over a wide strain-rate range from static to seismic using three-point bending tests combined with acoustic-emission monitoring. Peak load increased by up to 37.1% and fracture energy by up to 36.7%, indicating a pseudo-strengthening effect. Microscopically, failure shifted from ductile interfacial cracking along the interfacial transition zone to brittle transgranular cracking that directly fractures aggregates. Acoustic-emission analysis also showed a shift from predominantly Mode I tensile cracking toward Mode II shear cracking, with the proportion of shear cracks increasing from 16.2% to 53.8%. The findings highlight a trade-off between apparent fracture-strength enhancement and ductility reduction under rapid loading, with implications for seismic engineering and structural-safety assessment.

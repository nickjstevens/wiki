# Stablecoins

## Definition
Stablecoins are on-chain assets designed to maintain a relatively stable reference value, most commonly against the US dollar.

## Why they matter
In this source batch, stablecoins are the clearest bridge between crypto market structure and macro dollar liquidity. ARK presents them as major settlement infrastructure rather than a niche crypto product.

## Scale in 2024
ARK reports roughly $15.6T in annualized transaction value during 2024, more than Visa and Mastercard combined on the cited basis. It also notes supply near $203B, around 23 million active addresses in December 2024, and persistent dominance by dollar-pegged issuers.

## Macro significance
Stablecoins extend dollar-linked settlement into global on-chain markets and create a channel between blockchain adoption and traditional reserve assets such as US Treasuries. That makes them relevant not only to [[public-blockchains]] but also to broader liquidity conditions discussed in [[late-cycle-risk-cascade]].

## Dollar-system and geopolitical angle
The Hormuz toll source is not about stablecoins directly, but it raises the same settlement question from the opposite side: if oil chokepoint payments migrate toward yuan, rial, bitcoin, or other nontraditional rails, dollar-linked stablecoins should be watched as part of the broader contest between dollar liquidity, alternative settlement assets, and crisis payment infrastructure.

## Risks and open questions
- reserve quality and regulation
- concentration among major issuers
- whether usage reflects real commerce, trading, or both

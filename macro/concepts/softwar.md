# Softwar

## Definition
Softwar is Jason Lowery's thesis that proof-of-work, especially in [[bitcoin]], should be understood as cyber power projection that imposes real-world physical costs in cyberspace.

## Core claim
The paper argues that proof-of-work is not merely a monetary innovation. It is presented as a security mechanism that physically constrains computation through energy expenditure and therefore changes how cyberspace can be defended or contested.

## Why it belongs in this wiki
This is a high-conviction interpretive thesis, not neutral consensus doctrine. It matters because it extends Bitcoin analysis beyond money, liquidity, and market structure into strategic and state-power language.

## Non-consensus status
This page should be read as a map of Lowery's argument rather than as settled fact. Its importance is explanatory: it shows one strong narrative competing with more standard framings of [[public-blockchains]] and more rights-oriented framings such as [[financial-freedom-under-authoritarianism]].

# Bitcoin Quantum Risk

## Definition
Bitcoin quantum risk is the possibility that future cryptographically relevant quantum computers could undermine elliptic-curve assumptions used in parts of the Bitcoin ecosystem.

## ARK/Unchained framing
The ARK/Unchained paper argues the risk is real but not imminent. It frames the problem as staged, observable, and gradual rather than a sudden one-day collapse, and argues current hardware remains well below the threshold needed to threaten Bitcoin at scale.

## Exposure and mitigation
The paper distinguishes between already-exposed address types, migratable funds, and future upgrade paths. It also notes that ecosystem work is already underway on mitigation, wallet design, and post-quantum migration ideas.

## Timeline disagreement
The HRF report carries a more urgent tone and cites expert views that cryptographically relevant systems could arrive within a handful of years. That does not necessarily refute the ARK/Unchained analysis, but it does create a meaningful disagreement in urgency and framing.

## Why this matters
Because Bitcoin is treated in these sources as both savings infrastructure and freedom infrastructure, quantum risk is not only a technical issue. It also affects [[financial-freedom-under-authoritarianism]] and the institutional role of [[human-rights-foundation]].

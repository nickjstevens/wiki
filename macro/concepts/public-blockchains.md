# Public Blockchains

## Definition
Public blockchains are open networks for recording ownership, settlement, and programmable transaction logic without relying on a single central administrator.

## ARK's platform view
ARK presents public blockchains as a foundational innovation platform capable of hosting money, contracts, and internet-native financial infrastructure. In this framing, lower costs, open access, and programmable assets matter as much as censorship-resistance.

## Network competition and scaling
The ARK report highlights the concentration of cost-sensitive activity on Solana L1 and Ethereum L2s, and notes that EIP-4844 materially reduced L2 transaction costs. This positions public blockchains as competitive infrastructure rather than a monolithic stack.

## Tension with proof-of-work framing
[[softwar]] pushes a narrower but more strategic argument: some blockchain systems, especially proof-of-work systems such as [[bitcoin]], may also be analyzed as security infrastructure rather than only financial rails.

## Open questions
- Which functions belong on general-purpose chains versus specialized rails?
- How much of blockchain adoption is driven by speculation versus real settlement demand?
- To what extent do open financial rails depend on dollar-linked instruments such as [[stablecoins]]?

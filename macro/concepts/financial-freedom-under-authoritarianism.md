# Financial Freedom Under Authoritarianism

## Definition
This topic covers how states or authorities can weaponize money, banking access, surveillance, demonetization, confiscation, and account control, and why Bitcoin is presented as one response to those pressures.

## HRF framing
HRF's report is the clearest source in this batch on this topic. It argues that many dissidents, activists, nonprofits, and politically exposed individuals are constrained not only by censorship of speech but also by censorship of money.

## Why Bitcoin is central
In this framing, [[bitcoin]] matters because it can support self-custody, border-crossing transfers, and donation or savings rails outside conventional account-based systems. This is a practical rather than purely ideological argument.

## Relation to Softwar
[[softwar]] broadens the discussion from civil-liberties use cases to strategic state-level power. The overlap is that both sources treat Bitcoin as infrastructure rather than merely a tradeable asset.

## Limits and caveats
Bitcoin does not eliminate operational, legal, education, or security risks. The value of the tool depends on user competence, surrounding infrastructure, and threat model.

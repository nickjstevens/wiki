# Late-Cycle Risk Cascade

## Definition
Late-cycle risk cascade is Benjamin Cowen's staged framework for how tightening liquidity and fragility propagate through markets before feeding into the real economy.

## Staged model
The framework moves from declining speculative participation, to weakness in highly speculative assets, to deterioration in traditional risk assets, to tighter financial conditions, and finally to labor-market feedback.

## Why crypto matters early
Cowen treats crypto weakness, especially altcoin underperformance relative to [[bitcoin]], as an early warning sign rather than an isolated sector event. This makes crypto part of the macro transmission path.

## Macro links
The report ties the process to defensive rotation, a stronger dollar, higher oil prices, and eventual labor-market deterioration. In that sense it connects naturally to dollar-linked rails such as [[stablecoins]] and to broader market-timing questions such as [[cycle-trading-patterns]].

## Usefulness and limitation
The framework is useful because it is multi-factor rather than single-cause. Its limitation is that it offers sequence and interpretation more than precise timing.
